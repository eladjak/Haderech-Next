## Key Insights from Examples

### What Makes a High Score?
1. **Empathy**: Use understanding phrases + questions + emotional words
2. **Clarity**: Structure (first, second), examples, good length (10-80 words)
3. **Effectiveness**: Action words (let's, suggest) + solution words (plan, fix)
4. **Appropriateness**: Professional tone, avoid slang/casual language
5. **Professionalism**: Formal language, no attacks, no caps lock
6. **Problem-Solving**: Identify problem + propose solution + ask why

### What Makes a Low Score?
1. **Empathy**: Commands (must, should), no questions, directive
2. **Clarity**: Too short (< 5 words), filler words (umm, like), too long
3. **Effectiveness**: Vague (maybe, perhaps), passive, no action
4. **Appropriateness**: Inappropriate words, casual slang (lol, omg, wtf)
5. **Professionalism**: Personal attacks, caps lock, slang (gonna, yeah)
6. **Problem-Solving**: Complaining without solutions, no analysis
